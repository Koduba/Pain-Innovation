Pharmaceutical Intelligence Overview

Real-world safety surveillance · Post-marketing monitoring · 18 pain medications tracked · ICH E2C/E2E compliant

Why This Changes Pharmacovigilance

Traditional pharmacovigilance relies on spontaneous adverse event databases (FAERS, EudraVigilance) that lack denominators · you know how many people reported an event, but not how many were exposed without event. PIN solves this. Because we track every patient longitudinally · drug start dates, doses, co-medications, phenotype cluster, and outcomes · we can calculate true incidence rates: events per 1,000 person-years of exposure. We also know which patients are at risk by cluster type, enabling the first phenotype-stratified signal detection in pain pharmacovigilance. This data is directly sellable to pharma sponsors as post-authorization safety studies (PASS) under EMA Article 107 and FDA FDAAA 801 requirements.

PV Architecture · How PIN Detects Signals
Longitudinal Exposure with Denominator
Unlike FAERS, every patient has start/stop dates, dose records, and concurrent medications. True incidence rates · not just case counts.
3-Algorithm Signal Ensemble
PRR + ROR + EBGM (MGPS). Signal confirmed only when ≥2 algorithms exceed threshold simultaneously. Reduces false positives.
Phenotype-Stratified Detection
Neurobehavioral cluster data enables the world's first phenotype-stratified signal detection. Type A patients may show signals invisible in aggregate data.
Automated Regulatory Preparation
PSUR/PBRER data packages auto-assembled from registry. Reduces safety report preparation from weeks to days.

Three-Algorithm Ensemble Methodology

PIN uses a confirmed-signal framework requiring ≥2 of 3 algorithms to exceed threshold simultaneously: PRR ≥2.0 with Ï‡²≥4 and n≥3; ROR with lower bound of 95% CI ≥1.0; EBGM ≥2.0 from the Multi-item Gamma Poisson Shrinker. The critical PIN advantage: all algorithms are run against a denominator-based dataset, enabling calculation of excess cases above expected background · the gold standard regulators are increasingly demanding for label updates and REMS decisions.

Benefit-Risk Assessment

Structured B-R evaluation · PROTECT methodology · Patient-level longitudinal data · Regulatory submission ready

The PIN Benefit-Risk Advantage

Traditional benefit-risk relies on clinical trial data for benefits and spontaneous reports for risks · two incompatible, non-overlapping datasets. PIN is the first real-world platform where both sides come from the same longitudinal cohort. We know if a patient's pain improved on Drug X (benefit) AND if they experienced an adverse event (risk), measured at the same time points, in the same person, stratified by their neurobehavioral cluster. This enables true patient-level benefit-risk calculation · the methodology EMA's PROTECT initiative and FDA's Patient-Focused Drug Development (PFDD) framework explicitly call for.
